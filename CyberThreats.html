Cyber Threats information
There are a variety of cyber threats that can affect students at Milligan Two types of threats that are important for students to be aware of is malware and identity theft.

Malware is unwanted software that can damage a computer system. Criminals can use malware to steal personal or sensitive information, implement unwanted ads, or do other malicious things.

How do you know your computer is affected by malware?
Your computer may...
  • Not allow you to remove software
  • Suddenly slow down or crash
  • Prohibit restart or or shut down
  • Quicky drain battery
How could malware get on your device?
  • Clicking on links that happen to have fake security pop-ups sent by tech-support scammers.
  • Phishing emails
  • Prohibit restart or or shut down
  • Downloading content on file-sharing sites
How can I prevent malware attacks?
  • Clicking on links that happen to have fake security pop-ups sent by tech-support scammers.
  • Phishing emails
  • Prohibit restart or or shut down
  • Downloading content on file-sharing sites
 Identity Theft
Identity theft can lead to damage of credit rating or credit score, stealing of tax refunds, accusations of arrest, unwnanted use of health insurance, and can open an account under your name How can you detect that you have been a victim of identity theft?
You can
  • Check bank account statements
  • Review Bills
  • Track bills you owe and when they are due
  • Get and review credit reports

Your social security number is important. Before giving it to someone else ask
  • Why do you need it?
  • How will you protect it?
  • Could you use just those four digits?
  • Can you use a different identifier?
 There are five types of insider threats: intentional, uninterntional, third-party, malicious, and collusive
Intentional Threat
An intentional threat is where someone inside of an organization has malicious intent and wants to harm an organization for personal grievances or personal benefit
Unintentional Threats
An unintentional threat is where someone inside of an organization harms the organziation without intending to do
There are two types of insiders that can pose unintentional threats
  • Negligent Insiders: ignore security policies even though they are aware of them
  • Accidental insiders: People who harm an organization without meaning to such as clicking on phishing emails or typing the wrong email address
Don't let this be you!
Malicious threats is a form of intentional threat that may cause harm or an act of vengeance
People who use these threats tend to aim at sensitive data to advance their careers or sabotage corporate systems for business competition
Malicious insider threats could come from rival companies that may pay hackers to help with their businesses. The rival companies may be financially motivated. A collusive insider threat is a type of malicious insider therat where one insider is working with an external partner to compromize an organization for money purposes. Third party insider threats usually happen when a business partner or contractor tries to invade an organization's security. This will come from people who aren't part of the company.This threat can happen directly or indirectly.
